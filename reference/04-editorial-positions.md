# WhoAdvice: Editorial Positions

## 1. Research-led means research-led

WhoAdvice currently bases recommendations on research, customer reviews, Reddit discussions, and competitor analysis.

Therefore, use language such as:

- We researched
- We compared
- We analyzed available specifications
- We reviewed recurring customer feedback
- We selected products using the criteria below

Do not use:

- We tested
- In our tests
- Our testers found
- After wearing it
- We used it for weeks

unless genuine testing records are provided.

## 2. “Best” is conditional

No product is universally best.

A “Best Overall” product must:

- Perform well across the article’s most important criteria
- Suit a broad part of the target audience
- Have no disqualifying safety, reliability, or value problem
- Beat the alternatives for a clearly documented reason

Other award labels should identify a real use case:

- Best for fine hair
- Best for sensitive skin
- Best budget device
- Best for travel
- Best fragrance-free option

Avoid decorative labels created only to include another affiliate product.

## 3. A ranking is an editorial conclusion

A high retailer rating does not automatically produce a high ranking.

Rankings should weigh:

- Fit for the search intent
- Verified features
- Evidence quality
- Recurring user experience
- Drawbacks
- Value
- Availability
- Safety and maintenance
- Meaningful differences from other picks

## 4. Customer reviews are useful but limited

Customer reviews can support claims about:

- Texture
- Scent
- Packaging
- Ease of use
- Comfort
- Learning curve
- Durability reports
- Common complaints
- Differences across hair or skin types

Customer reviews cannot by themselves prove:

- Clinical efficacy
- Medical safety
- Disease treatment
- Ingredient concentration
- Cause and effect
- Universal compatibility

Look for repeated patterns across recent reviews. Do not select one dramatic comment as representative.

## 5. Reddit is a research input, not an authority

Use Reddit to discover:

- Questions readers actually ask
- Terminology used by real consumers
- Unexpected complaints
- Long-term ownership issues
- Product comparisons
- Routine and technique differences
- Gaps in competing articles

Do not use Reddit as the sole source for medical, safety, scientific, or product-specification claims.

Do not imply that a handful of comments represent all users.

## 6. Competitor articles are discovery sources

Competitor research may reveal:

- Products worth investigating
- Common comparison criteria
- Questions competitors answer or ignore
- Experts, studies, or documents to locate independently
- Changes in the market

Competitor articles must not be the final authority for:

- Product specifications
- Ingredients
- Clinical claims
- Quotes
- Prices
- Test results
- Rankings

Verify the underlying fact from a stronger source.

Never paraphrase a competitor paragraph sentence by sentence.

## 7. Manufacturer information requires attribution

Manufacturer pages and manuals are authoritative for:

- Product name
- Model
- Included accessories
- Dimensions
- Instructions
- Warranty terms
- Official ingredient list
- Published performance claims

They are not independent proof that the product performs as claimed.

Use:

> The brand says the formula provides 24-hour hydration.

Not:

> The formula provides 24-hour hydration.

unless an independent source supports the statement.

## 8. Ingredient presence is not outcome proof

The presence of an ingredient can justify explaining its established cosmetic function, but it does not prove that the finished product produces a result.

A formula’s effect depends on factors including concentration, delivery, stability, supporting ingredients, use, and individual response.

Do not write “contains X, therefore it will Y.”

## 9. Health and medical boundaries

WhoAdvice may explain general information from qualified sources. It must not:

- Diagnose
- Prescribe
- Tell a reader to stop prescribed treatment
- Guarantee relief
- Call a cosmetic a cure
- Recommend using a device against its safety instructions
- Minimize a serious reaction

Use a short professional-care note where relevant. Avoid generic disclaimers in every paragraph.

## 10. Honest negatives improve trust

Do not hide drawbacks because a product is a top pick.

A useful con is:

- Repeated in credible feedback
- Visible in verified specifications
- A reasonable consequence of the design
- Relevant to a distinct user group
- Important to value or safety

Do not manufacture a trivial con to make a review look balanced.

## 11. Price is part of the verdict, not proof of quality

Judge value through:

- Price per ounce or unit
- Included accessories
- Replacement costs
- Expected product usage
- Warranty
- Versatility
- Availability of cheaper alternatives

A premium product must have a specific advantage that matters to the target reader.

## 12. Sales coverage must remain editorial

A discount is not a reason to recommend a poor fit.

Before including a deal, verify:

- Current sale price
- Typical recent price when available
- Seller
- Model or variant
- End date or limited-time status
- Whether the product was already editorially recommendable

Avoid false urgency and unverified “lowest price ever” claims.

## 13. Inclusive product guidance

Do not treat one skin tone, hair texture, age group, or body type as the default.

When relevant, evaluate:

- White cast across skin tones
- Shade availability
- Curl and coil patterns
- Fine, medium, and coarse strands
- Sensitive or reactive skin
- Grip and control for different users
- Accessibility of packaging or device controls

Do not claim universal inclusivity from marketing language alone.

## 14. “Clean,” “natural,” and similar terms

Do not use “clean beauty,” “non-toxic,” “chemical-free,” or “natural” as safety conclusions.

When a brand uses one of these terms:

- Attribute it to the brand
- Explain the specific standard or exclusion list when available
- Prefer concrete facts such as “fragrance-free” or “formulated without X”
- Do not imply that an alternative formula is harmful

## 15. Certifications and ethical claims

Claims such as vegan, cruelty-free, organic, recyclable, reef-friendly, or sustainable require:

- Current official brand documentation, and
- A recognized certification when the wording implies certification

Do not convert “not tested on animals” into “certified cruelty-free.”

## 16. Corrections

When a material error is discovered:

1. Correct the article.
2. Record what changed and the date.
3. Recheck dependent claims, rankings, tables, and metadata.
4. Update the verdict if the correction changes the recommendation.

Accuracy is more important than preserving the original ranking.
