# WhoAdvice: Approved Claims and Sources

## Purpose

This file defines which sources may support which claims and how those claims should be worded.

A source can be authoritative for one fact and weak for another. A brand manual may be the best source for attachment count but not independent proof of comfort.

## 1. Approved source directory

### Regulators and public-safety authorities

Use for legal status, warnings, recalls, labeling, and regulated claims.

- U.S. Food and Drug Administration — `fda.gov`
- U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission — `cpsc.gov`
- Federal Trade Commission — `ftc.gov`
- European Commission CosIng database — `ec.europa.eu` or `single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu`
- Relevant national regulator for the target market

Useful FDA resources include:

- Cosmetics
- Cosmetics Labeling Claims
- Is It a Cosmetic, a Drug, or Both?
- Recalls, Market Withdrawals, and Safety Alerts
- Prohibited and Restricted Ingredients in Cosmetics

### Professional medical organizations

Use for general skin, hair, scalp, and sun-care guidance.

- American Academy of Dermatology — `aad.org`
- Other recognized professional organizations when the specialty matches the topic

Do not imply that an organization endorses a specific product unless it explicitly does.

### Research databases and journals

Use for ingredient, safety, efficacy, and mechanism research.

- PubMed — `pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov`
- Cochrane Library
- Peer-reviewed journals
- Systematic reviews and clinical guidelines

Prefer systematic reviews and well-designed human studies over isolated laboratory studies.

### Cosmetic ingredient references

Use with context and limitations.

- Cosmetic Ingredient Review — `cir-safety.org`
- European Commission CosIng
- Official INCI lists
- Current product labels
- Peer-reviewed formulation or dermatology literature

Do not treat a consumer ingredient-scoring website as a final safety authority.

### Product-specific primary sources

Use for exact product facts.

- Official brand product page
- Official manual
- Current packaging
- Warranty page
- Safety sheet where relevant
- Authorized retailer listing
- Official recall or support notice

### Consumer-experience sources

Use for patterns of experience, not scientific proof.

- Verified retailer reviews
- Reddit threads
- Specialist forums
- Long-term owner discussions
- Credible video demonstrations

### Competitor sources

Use only for discovery and coverage analysis.

- Established magazines
- Product-review sites
- Niche blogs
- Affiliate roundups

Locate and verify their underlying sources.

## 2. Claim-language ladder

### Level A: Direct verified fact

Requirements:

- Exact-model primary source or authoritative database
- No material conflict

Approved wording:

- The device includes...
- The label lists...
- The manual permits...
- The product carries an SPF...
- The warranty covers...

### Level B: Strong independent evidence

Requirements:

- Transparent independent test, systematic review, guideline, or suitable study

Approved wording:

- In the cited test...
- The study found...
- Dermatology guidance recommends...
- Independent testing reported...

Do not generalize beyond the evidence.

### Level C: Brand claim

Requirements:

- Current official brand or packaging source

Approved wording:

- The brand says...
- According to the manufacturer...
- The product is marketed as...
- The company reports...

Do not remove the attribution later in the paragraph.

### Level D: Review pattern

Requirements:

- Repeated pattern across a meaningful set of reviews
- Source and date recorded

Approved wording:

- Reviewers commonly mention...
- A recurring complaint concerns...
- Feedback is divided on...
- Buyers with fine hair are more likely to report...

### Level E: Editorial inference

Requirements:

- Reasonable interpretation of verified evidence
- Clearly presented as analysis

Approved wording:

- This should make it easier to...
- That design is likely to suit...
- On paper, it is the stronger option for...
- We consider it better value because...

Avoid presenting inference as measured performance.

### Level F: Anecdotal signal

Requirements:

- Reddit, forum, isolated review, or creator account

Approved wording:

- Some users report...
- Several Reddit discussions mention...
- This is an anecdotal concern rather than established evidence.
- The reports are worth considering, but they do not establish cause.

## 3. Claims by category

### Product specifications

Best sources:

1. Manual
2. Official product page
3. Packaging
4. Authorized retailer

Allowed:

> The manual lists 2 speed settings.

Not allowed:

> It offers precise professional-level speed control.

unless independently demonstrated.

### Ingredient presence

Best sources:

1. Current packaging
2. Official regional ingredient list
3. Authorized retailer image or listing

Allowed:

> The current ingredient list includes glycerin and niacinamide.

Not allowed:

> Its high concentration of niacinamide...

unless concentration is disclosed.

### Ingredient function

Best sources:

- Professional medical guidance
- Cosmetic Ingredient Review
- Peer-reviewed research
- Regulatory references

Allowed:

> Glycerin is commonly used as a humectant, helping formulas attract and retain water.

Do not promise the finished product’s result from this fact alone.

### Efficacy

Best sources:

- Independent controlled testing
- Peer-reviewed human research
- Transparent consumer test
- Qualified expert assessment

If only a brand claim exists:

> The brand reports improved hydration after 24 hours.

Do not write:

> It clinically improves hydration.

without the actual study and relevant details.

### Safety

Best sources:

- Regulator
- Official warnings
- Systematic review
- Qualified medical organization
- Product manual

Avoid blanket “safe” claims. State the condition of use.

Preferred:

> The manual says not to use the device on broken or irritated skin.

### Suitability for sensitive skin

Approved evidence:

- Fragrance-free formula
- Relevant test details
- Current label
- Qualified expert guidance
- Consistent review evidence

Do not equate “dermatologist tested” with “approved by dermatologists.”

Preferred:

> It is fragrance-free and the brand reports testing on sensitive skin, but individual reactions remain possible.

### Non-comedogenic

Use only when:

- The brand or label makes the claim, or
- Suitable independent testing is available

Attribute the claim. Do not infer it from the ingredient list.

### Hypoallergenic

Treat as a brand or label claim, not a guarantee that no reaction will occur.

### Cruelty-free

Accept:

- Current recognized certification
- Clear current brand policy

State the precise status:

- Certified by [organization]
- Brand states it does not test finished products on animals

Do not broaden the claim to suppliers or parent companies without evidence.

### Vegan

Verify the current product, not only the brand. Avoid assuming that cruelty-free means vegan.

### Organic

Use the specific certification and scope. “Made with organic ingredients” is not the same as a fully certified organic product.

### Clean, non-toxic, chemical-free

Do not use as independent safety conclusions.

Replace with concrete facts:

- Fragrance-free
- Contains no added color
- Formulated without [specific ingredient]
- Meets [named certification]

### Dermatologist tested

Report only the exact claim.

Do not convert it to:

- Dermatologist approved
- Dermatologist recommended
- Clinically proven
- Safe for sensitive skin

### Clinically tested or clinically proven

Before using, obtain:

- Study design
- Sample size
- Duration
- Comparator
- Outcome
- Sponsor
- Publication or full brand report

If details are unavailable, write:

> The brand describes the product as clinically tested but does not publish enough detail for us to evaluate the study.

### Long-lasting, all-day, or 24-hour claims

Treat as brand claims unless independent evidence exists.

### Pain-free

Do not use for epilators, razors, laser devices, or similar tools unless a rigorous source supports the exact context. Prefer:

- Intended to reduce discomfort
- Reviewers report less discomfort than...
- Comfort feedback varies

### Hair growth or hair-loss claims

Require authoritative medical evidence. Cosmetics and routine products should not be described as treating hair loss without appropriate regulatory and clinical support.

### Acne, eczema, rosacea, psoriasis, or dermatitis

Do not claim a cosmetic cures or treats these conditions. Distinguish an OTC drug or prescription product from a cosmetic and follow authoritative guidance.

### SPF and sun protection

Verify the current label and market. Sunscreen regulation differs by jurisdiction.

Report:

- SPF number
- Broad-spectrum status
- Water-resistance duration
- Reapplication instructions

Do not infer SPF from ingredients or makeup marketing.

### Popularity

Allowed with data:

> The listing had more than 8,000 ratings when checked on June 28, 2026.

Avoid:

- Everyone loves it
- Internet-famous
- Cult favorite

unless the phrase is clearly contextual and supported.

### Value

Calculate:

- Cost per ounce
- Cost per use when reasonable
- Included attachments
- Replacement cost
- Warranty
- Product longevity evidence

Approved:

> At the checked price, it costs about $4 less per ounce than the next-cheapest option.

### Durability

Use:

- Warranty
- Material or construction facts
- Long-term review patterns
- Independent tests

Avoid declaring a product durable from appearance.

## 4. Review and Reddit wording

Approved:

- Among recent reviews we examined...
- A repeated complaint is...
- Feedback is mixed...
- Several users with fine hair say...
- Reddit discussions raise a possible issue with...
- These reports are anecdotal.

Not approved:

- Customers prove...
- Reddit confirms...
- Users universally agree...
- This always causes...
- No one experiences...

## 5. Affiliate and endorsement disclosure

WhoAdvice should maintain a clear affiliate disclosure near commercial content.

FTC guidance requires material connections to be disclosed clearly and conspicuously. Do not bury the disclosure in a general policy page when the context requires a nearby disclosure.

When Amazon Associates links appear, include Amazon's required associate-identification statement, or a currently permitted substantially similar statement, clearly and prominently on the site. The article-level disclosure must remain near the first commercial content even when a sitewide disclosure also exists.

Treat affiliate-link implementation as a controlled publishing field:

- Use only publisher-generated Special Links for the approved account and market.
- Match each link to the exact model, variant, and package reviewed.
- Preserve the raw retailer URL and ASIN separately from the affiliate URL so identity can be audited.
- Qualify published affiliate links with `rel="sponsored"`; `nofollow` may be added under site policy but does not replace `sponsored` as the preferred relationship label.
- Recheck the redirect, selected variation, seller, fulfillment, stock, and displayed price at publication.
- Do not copy live prices, availability, ratings, review counts, or Amazon-hosted images into evergreen prose unless the publishing system has a compliant, dated refresh process.

Editorial copy must remain truthful regardless of commission.

Current primary references:

- FTC Endorsement Guides and disclosure guidance: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/topics/truth-advertising/advertisement-endorsements
- Amazon Associates Operating Agreement: https://affiliate-program.amazon.com/help/operating/agreement/
- Amazon SiteStripe link-generation help: https://affiliate-program.amazon.com/help/node/topic/GJMMT7G4C8K4Y3AY
- Google outbound-link qualification guidance: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/qualify-outbound-links

## 6. Sources that cannot stand alone

Do not use the following as sole support for a consequential claim:

- Search-result snippets
- AI-generated summaries
- Competitor roundups
- Unsourced infographics
- Retailer question-and-answer sections
- Influencer sponsorships
- One customer review
- One Reddit comment
- Marketplace seller copy
- Anonymous “expert” statements
