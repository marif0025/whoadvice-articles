# WhoAdvice: Fact-Checking Policy

## Core rule

Every factual statement must be:

- Verified,
- Attributed,
- Clearly presented as analysis, or
- Removed.

Plausibility is not verification.

## 1. Build a claim ledger

Maintain a claim ledger during research and drafting.

```markdown
| Claim | Type | Source | Source date | Evidence strength | Wording allowed | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Device is shower-safe | specification | official manual | 2026-05-10 | high | direct fact | verified |
| Formula lasts 24 hours | brand claim | brand page | 2026-05-10 | medium | "brand says" | verified |
| Pump often clogs | review pattern | 2 retailers | 2026-05-10 | medium | attributed pattern | verified |
| Treats eczema | medical claim | none | — | none | not allowed | remove |
```

Claims that materially affect the ranking must appear in the ledger.

## 2. Claim types

### Product identity

Examples:

- Product name
- Model
- Variant
- Generation
- Size
- Color
- Included bundle

Verify from the brand, manual, packaging, or consistent retailer information.

### Specifications

Examples:

- Runtime
- Charge time
- Wet/dry use
- Attachment count
- Dimensions
- Warranty
- Temperature
- SPF rating

Prefer official documentation. Check that the specification applies to the exact model.

### Formula and ingredients

Verify the current formula from:

1. Product packaging or official ingredient list
2. Current brand page
3. Authorized retailer when the official source is unavailable

Record formula date or page-access date. Do not combine ingredient lists from different markets.

### Performance

Independent evidence is preferred. If only the brand supports the claim, attribute it.

### User experience

Use review or forum patterns with clear attribution. Do not present anecdotes as controlled findings.

### Health and safety

Use regulators, qualified professional organizations, systematic reviews, peer-reviewed research, or named qualified experts.

### Price and availability

Record:

- Price
- Retailer
- Seller
- Variant
- Date and time checked
- Discount conditions

Prices are snapshots, not permanent facts.

## 3. Source hierarchy

### Tier 1: Primary authoritative sources

- Government regulators
- Official product manuals and labels
- Standards organizations
- Peer-reviewed systematic reviews
- Professional medical organizations
- Recall databases
- Certification bodies

### Tier 2: Strong original sources

- Peer-reviewed studies
- Qualified expert interviews
- Transparent independent laboratory tests
- Brand documentation for product-specific facts
- Authorized retailer listings for current stock and price

### Tier 3: User-experience sources

- Verified-purchase reviews
- Reddit
- Forums
- Creator demonstrations
- Social posts

### Tier 4: Discovery-only sources

- Competitor buying guides
- General blogs
- Search snippets
- AI summaries
- Affiliate roundups without methods

A Tier 4 source should lead to a stronger source, not become the final citation.

## 4. Two-source rule

Use at least two independent sources for a consequential claim when:

- The sources conflict
- The claim affects safety
- The claim changes the ranking
- The claim is surprising
- The product has multiple similar models
- The manufacturer’s wording is unclear
- A review pattern may be seller-specific

A manufacturer page and its copied Amazon listing are not independent sources.

## 5. Exact-model rule

Before using a fact, match:

- Brand
- Product line
- Model number
- Generation
- Region
- Size
- Bundle
- Publication date

Never mix specifications across variants.

## 6. Formula-change rule

Cosmetic formulas can change.

When sources disagree:

1. Prefer packaging or the brand’s current regional page.
2. Check whether the retailer image is stale.
3. Record both lists.
4. Do not state the disputed ingredient as fact.
5. Add an editor note to verify current packaging.

## 7. Ingredient-evidence rule

Do not infer finished-product results from ingredient reputation.

For an ingredient claim, verify:

- The ingredient is actually in the current formula.
- The source discusses the same route of use and relevant outcome.
- The wording reflects the evidence level.
- The article does not imply a disclosed concentration when none is available.
- Safety conclusions account for formulation and use, not merely presence.

## 8. Medical fact protocol

For claims about acne, eczema, dermatitis, hair loss, allergies, infections, burns, hormones, pregnancy, or disease:

1. Find an authoritative medical source.
2. Confirm the publication or update date.
3. Distinguish general education from individual advice.
4. Avoid diagnosing the reader.
5. Include limits and contraindications.
6. Recommend professional advice where symptoms are severe, persistent, sudden, or worsening.

Do not rely on a beauty brand or retailer for medical guidance.

## 9. Quote verification

Before publishing a quote:

- Open the original source.
- Verify the exact wording.
- Verify the speaker’s name and credentials.
- Confirm the quote has not been taken out of context.
- Keep the quote short.
- Record the source URL and date.
- Do not quote a competitor’s quote without locating the original interview when possible.

Never fabricate or polish a quote.

## 10. Review-pattern verification

A valid review pattern requires more than one comment.

Record:

- Source
- Approximate sample reviewed
- Total available review count
- Date
- Variant
- Positive pattern
- Negative pattern
- Contradictory feedback
- Potential seller or shipping issues

Use language such as “a recurring complaint” only when recurrence is actually observed.

## 11. Statistics

For every statistic, record:

- Original source
- Study or dataset
- Population
- Date
- Relevant limitation

Do not cite a secondary article when the primary dataset is available.

Do not convert a laboratory result into a universal consumer outcome.

## 12. Current-information checks

Immediately before publication, recheck:

- Product availability
- Price
- Discount
- Model
- Formula
- Recall status
- Warranty
- Seller
- Certification
- Expert role or affiliation when relevant

Use absolute dates in internal notes.

## 13. Conflict resolution

When credible sources disagree:

1. Identify whether they refer to different variants, regions, dates, or definitions.
2. Prefer the source closest to the fact.
3. Describe the disagreement if it matters.
4. Reduce claim confidence.
5. Do not choose the version that creates the strongest marketing sentence.

Example:

> Retailer pages list different charge times for this model, and the current manual does not resolve the conflict. We have not used runtime as a ranking factor.

## 14. Unknowns

Unknown information must be labeled:

- Not disclosed
- Not verified
- Current sources conflict
- No independent evidence located
- Review evidence is too limited

Do not fill gaps with “likely” unless the statement is explicitly editorial analysis and the basis is given.

## 15. Safety and recall check

For relevant products, search:

- FDA recalls and safety alerts
- CPSC recalls
- Official manufacturer notices
- Regional regulator databases
- Current product warnings

A recall affecting the exact model must be escalated before recommendation.

## 16. Plagiarism and originality check

- Do not copy competitor phrasing.
- Do not closely paraphrase a source paragraph.
- Keep source notes separate from draft prose.
- Rewrite from verified facts and editorial analysis.
- Use short quotations only when exact wording matters.
- Check distinctive phrases before publication.

## 17. Prepublication fact audit

The fact checker must confirm:

- Every product is the current intended variant.
- Product names and model numbers match.
- Strong claims are cited or attributed.
- Brand claims remain labeled as brand claims.
- Review patterns are not exaggerated.
- Prices include a date.
- Tables match the prose.
- Pros and cons match the evidence packet.
- No first-person testing was invented.
- Health statements use authoritative sources.
- Recall checks are complete.
- Unknowns were not silently converted into facts.

## 18. Correction policy

For a factual error:

- Correct it promptly.
- Add a correction note when the error was material.
- Recalculate scores and rankings.
- Recheck internal links and structured data.
- Record the correction date and source.
