# WhoAdvice: Brand and Audience

## Project purpose

WhoAdvice publishes practical, research-led guidance for people choosing products and routines in:

- Skin care
- Hair care
- Personal care

The site should help readers make a decision, not merely give them more products to browse.

Every article must answer three questions:

1. What is the reader trying to solve?
2. Which option is most suitable for that need?
3. What evidence supports the recommendation?

## Editorial promise

WhoAdvice gives readers clear product guidance based on:

- Verified product specifications and ingredient lists
- Manufacturer documentation
- Qualified expert guidance and reliable scientific sources
- Analysis of customer-review patterns
- Relevant Reddit discussions and other first-person user reports
- Comparison with competing products and current market options

WhoAdvice does **not** claim to operate a testing laboratory or to have personally used a product unless genuine first-hand testing notes are supplied for that article.

## Core brand position

WhoAdvice is a careful shopping guide, not a product catalog and not a beauty marketing channel.

The brand should feel:

- Approachable without sounding casual or careless
- Informed without sounding academic
- Confident without pretending certainty
- Enthusiastic without becoming promotional
- Practical without reducing every issue to a simplistic answer
- Honest about evidence gaps

## Primary audience

The core reader is an everyday shopper who:

- Wants a useful answer without reading a technical paper
- May be unfamiliar with cosmetic ingredients or device specifications
- Is comparing several products before spending money
- Wants to know who a product is for, not only what it contains
- Cares about comfort, ease of use, value, drawbacks, and realistic results
- May have sensitive skin, textured hair, acne-prone skin, dryness, irritation, or other specific needs
- Is likely to check Amazon reviews, Reddit, social media, and competing buying guides before purchasing

Do not assume the reader is an expert. Do not speak down to the reader.

## Reader jobs to be done

An article should help the reader do one or more of the following:

- Choose the best product for a defined use case
- Compare meaningful differences between similar products
- Understand which features matter and which are mostly marketing
- Avoid a product that conflicts with their needs
- Set realistic expectations
- Learn how to use a product more effectively
- Understand common complaints before buying
- Decide whether a premium price is justified
- Identify when professional medical advice is more appropriate than a product recommendation

## Audience priorities by category

### Skin care

Readers usually care about:

- Skin type and sensitivity
- Texture and finish
- Fragrance
- Active and supporting ingredients
- Compatibility with an existing routine
- Irritation risk
- Packaging and stability
- Claims such as hydration, brightening, acne care, redness support, or sun protection
- Price per ounce and expected frequency of use

### Hair care

Readers usually care about:

- Hair type, density, porosity, and curl pattern
- Moisture, hold, definition, slip, volume, and frizz control
- Weight, buildup, flakes, residue, and crunch
- Heat protection
- Color-treated or chemically processed hair
- Scalp sensitivity
- Scent
- Ease of styling and washout
- Value for the amount used per application

### Personal care

Readers usually care about:

- Comfort and irritation
- Ease of use
- Time required
- Hygiene and maintenance
- Wet or dry use
- Battery life, charging, and portability for devices
- Replacement parts and recurring costs
- Suitability for sensitive areas
- Noise, grip, storage, and cleaning
- Safety instructions and contraindications

## The trust contract

WhoAdvice must never:

- Imply hands-on testing that did not happen
- Turn a manufacturer claim into an established fact
- Present customer reviews or Reddit comments as scientific proof
- Hide a recurring product complaint
- Recommend a product only because it has a high commission
- Invent an expert, quote, test result, ingredient concentration, award, certification, or specification
- Diagnose a condition or replace medical advice
- Use confidence that exceeds the available evidence

When evidence is incomplete, say so plainly.

## Editorial independence

Commercial considerations must not determine the ranking.

A product may be included only when it earns a place through the article’s stated criteria. Affiliate availability can affect which retailer link is used, but it must not change:

- The score
- The verdict
- The pros and cons
- The assigned use case
- The order of recommendations

## Scope boundaries

WhoAdvice may discuss general care, product use, and shopping considerations. It must not provide individualized diagnosis or treatment.

Escalate or add a professional-care note when a topic involves:

- Persistent, severe, or worsening skin symptoms
- Suspected allergy or infection
- Sudden or unexplained hair loss
- Burns, wounds, or serious device reactions
- Prescription treatments
- Pregnancy-related safety questions without authoritative guidance
- Claims to cure, prevent, or treat disease

## Originality requirement

The project is informed by the high-level editorial traits observed in the supplied magazine articles: conversational framing, practical specificity, concise product explanations, and careful attribution.

Generated WhoAdvice articles must be original. Do not mention, impersonate, or copy the wording of any reference writer or publication. Do not reproduce distinctive sentences, metaphors, headlines, or paragraph structures from the samples.
