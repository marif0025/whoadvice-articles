## Which corded hedge trimmer type fits your yard?

Corded models draw continuous household power, but blade length and added cutting features change how they feel. Pick the simplest format that reaches and controls your actual hedge.

### Standard 22-inch trimmers

A 22-inch blade covers a broad hedge face in fewer passes and reaches across deeper shrubs. This format suits a long, flat boundary hedge in a small or medium yard. The extra length can feel awkward around tight corners and ornamental shapes.

### Compact 16-to-18-inch trimmers

A shorter blade brings the cutting action closer to your hands and improves placement in narrow gaps. It makes sense for two ornamental shrubs beside a porch. Wide hedge faces take longer because each sweep covers less area.

### Saw-tip hybrid trimmers

A saw tip gives the tool a separate way to address an occasional branch that exceeds the normal tooth gap. It suits mixed maintenance where a few thicker stems interrupt otherwise routine shaping. The higher purchase price is difficult to justify if you already own suitable loppers or a pruning saw.

## How to choose the best corded hedge trimmer for your yard

A corded tool is a layout decision before it is a motor decision. Measure the job and price the complete setup before comparing small specification differences.

### Map the outlet-to-hedge route

Measure from a suitable outdoor outlet to the farthest point you must trim. Follow the safe route the cable will actually take because corners, gates, paths, and separate beds add distance.

**Decision rule:** If the safe cable route is nearly as complicated as the trimming job, buy cordless.

### Budget for the extension cord

Most corded hedge trimmers have only a short power cord. Add the price of an outdoor-rated extension cord with the gauge and maximum length required by the tool manual. CPSC says outdoor extension cords need safety characteristics including suitable wire size, strain relief, polarization, continuity, and jacketed construction.

### Match blade length to hedge width

A 22-inch blade is efficient on broad, flat hedge faces. A 16-inch blade is easier to position around smaller shrubs and detailed shapes. More blade is not automatically better if the extra length makes the tool awkward in the spaces you maintain.

### Treat cut capacity as a limit, not a target

A 3/4-inch rating describes the tool's stated maximum capacity. It does not mean you should force the trimmer through every woody branch of that size. Regularly thick branches call for loppers, a pruning saw, or another suitable cutting tool. A hedge trimmer is primarily for shaping many smaller stems.

### Compare working weight and handles

Manufacturer-listed weights do not reveal balance, but they provide a useful first filter. A lighter tool can matter during vertical cuts and shoulder-height work. Wraparound or bale handles give your front hand more positions as you move between the hedge top and face.

### Check the safety controls

Look for a guard, two-hand or lock-off control, cord retention, and a blade sheath. Read the exact manual before buying because cord gauge, outlet protection, maintenance, and operating instructions can differ. A cord retainer prevents unplugging; it does not keep the cable away from the blade.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are corded hedge trimmers more powerful than cordless models?

Not necessarily. Amps and battery voltage describe different electrical systems and do not provide a direct performance comparison. Blade design, stroke rate, gearing, branch condition, and the complete tool all matter. Corded models mainly offer continuous runtime without a battery rather than a universal cutting-power advantage.

### What extension cord does a hedge trimmer need?

Use an outdoor-rated extension cord whose gauge and maximum length meet the exact tool manual. Longer runs may require thicker wire. Check the cord before each job, keep connections dry, and do not treat a household indoor cord as an acceptable substitute.

### Is a 22-inch blade too long?

A 22-inch blade is useful for broad hedge faces but can feel excessive around small shrubs and confined corners. Choose it when coverage and reach matter. A 16-to-18-inch model is usually easier to position for detailed shaping.

### Can a hedge trimmer cut 1-inch branches?

Use loppers or a pruning saw for regular 1-inch branches. Most standard corded trimmers in this guide are not designed for that work. CRAFTSMAN rates the CMEHTS8022 saw tip for occasional branches up to 1.5 inches.

### Is a corded hedge trimmer worth buying in 2026?

Yes, when the entire hedge is close to a suitable outlet and you value low purchase cost and uninterrupted runtime. Cordless is the better fit when cable routing is awkward, the property is large, or hedges sit far from an electrical outlet.
