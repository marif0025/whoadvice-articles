## Hedge-trimmer section contract

Lead with the job: use a hedge trimmer to shape quantities of fine outer growth across a hedge face or top.

Cover:

- Bulk shaping versus selective structural pruning
- Why tooth gap or manufacturer-stated capacity is not a guarantee of a clean cut through every wood type
- Why a thick isolated stem should be removed separately before surface trimming
- Early switch signals: the stem does not enter the blade opening correctly, the tool repeatedly hesitates, the growth deflects rather than cuts, or the exact manual's limit is approached
- If a blade stalls or jams, stop and isolate power exactly as the reader's manual requires before inspection or clearing

Do not make stalling the main or only tool-switch signal. Do not describe powered-tool isolation as one universal procedure because corded and battery models differ.

## Lopper section contract

Lead with the job: use loppers for one reachable woody stem that fits fully and cleanly inside the exact tool's jaws.

Cover:

- Two-handed leverage for individual selective cuts
- Exact-tool capacity and manufacturer instructions as the controlling limit
- Early switch signals: the branch does not seat properly, the handles cannot close cleanly, the cut requires twisting, the tool crushes rather than cuts, or access/branch movement is difficult to control

## Overgrown-hedge section contract

Keep renovation secondary to tool selection. Use approximately 180–260 words.

Use this decision sequence:

1. Identify the hedge and decide whether the job is maintenance or major reduction.
2. Inspect access, utilities, hidden obstructions, and the kinds of growth present.
3. Separate fine outer growth from individual woody stems.
4. Remove suitable woody stems selectively with loppers or a pruning saw.
5. Shape the remaining fine outer growth with a hedge trimmer.
6. Escalate major, uncertain, or species-sensitive renovation.

Allowed horticultural point:

- Major renovation may need staging, and some hedges may not regrow from old bare wood.

Controls:

## Pole tools, height, and professional-boundary contract

Lead with the distinction:

- A pole hedge trimmer extends a hedge-trimmer head for fine hedge growth at height.
- A pole saw places a saw on a pole for individual overhead woody branches.

They are not interchangeable.

Place the strongest professional-help warning in this section. Cover:

- Overhead utility lines: do not trim or saw near them; contact the utility and qualified line-clearance professionals
- Unstable, improvised, or ladder-dependent access
- Very large, loaded, or structurally uncertain branches
- Unclear drop zones or insufficient clearance
- Major structural reduction or work beyond the reader's training and physical control

Do not teach a ladder method, imply that a numeric distance authorizes homeowner work, or repeat the full professional warning in every earlier tool section. Short contextual cautions elsewhere are allowed only when needed.
