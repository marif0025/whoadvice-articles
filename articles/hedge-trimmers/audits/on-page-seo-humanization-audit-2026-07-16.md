# Hedge-trimmer on-page SEO and human-centered content audit

**Audit date:** July 16, 2026  
**Scope:** Seven published-draft `article.md` files in the hedge-trimmer cluster  
**Standard:** Updated `reference/on-page-seo-checklist.md`, `reference/02-voice-and-tone.md`, and `reference/16-human-centered-writing-and-editing.md`  
**Overall result:** **Pass after targeted revisions.** No article has a blocking search-intent, hierarchy, evidence-disclosure, or mechanical-format failure.

## How the ten humanization ideas are applied

The ten ideas are decision tools, not ten mandatory sections. Existing top-pick cards can satisfy the quick-summary requirement; Summary, Verdict, award, best-for, and caveat fields can satisfy “Who should buy this?”; and a comparison table can remove the need for repeated specification prose. Decision boxes, callouts, tips, scenarios, and visuals should be added only when they reduce decision effort without duplicating existing content.

5. Preserved all other components selectively; no identical “Who should buy this?” headings or decorative tip boxes were retrofitted across the cluster.
