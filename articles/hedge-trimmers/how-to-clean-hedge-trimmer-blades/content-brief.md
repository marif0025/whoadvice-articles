## Reader decision

The reader needs to remove debris and sticky residue without introducing liquid into the housing, damaging coatings or seals, creating a fire risk, or using an incompatible chemical. Cleaning and lubrication should finish with an inspection that routes dull blades to sharpening and bent, cracked, loose, or persistently binding blades to qualified service.

## Opening angle

Start with power isolation and the manual. Most routine care follows the same broad order—disconnect, brush away debris, remove permitted residue, dry, inspect, lubricate, and store—but the approved cleaner, lubricant, access points, and application method can vary by exact model.

## Chemical and lubricant boundaries

- Do not name a universal resin solvent, degreaser, disinfectant, oil, or spray.
- Do not recommend mixing chemicals or applying a flammable product near ignition sources without authoritative support.
- Do not suggest spraying directly into vents, motors, gear housings, electrical components, batteries, or hot engine parts.
- Do not claim that water is safe or unsafe for every tool; follow the exact manual's cleaning limits.
- Distinguish cleaning products from blade lubricants and gearbox grease.
- Do not turn a product's marketing claim into proof of material compatibility.
