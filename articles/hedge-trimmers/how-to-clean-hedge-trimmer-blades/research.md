## Research decision

**July 16 source refresh:** The current official US EGO HT2600/HT2600-FC manual was located and reopened. Its cleaning, water, resin-solvent, battery-removal, lubrication, frequency, and powered-distribution instructions match the captured evidence. The STIHL HSA 150 R/T and Makita XHU09 manuals, BLACK+DECKER support page, and STIHL SDS were also reopened and remain consistent with the draft. The EGO publication blocker is resolved; normal publication-day freshness and recall checks still apply.

Proceed with a dedicated cleaning and lubrication article, but do not publish one universal cleaner, water rule, lubricant, frequency, or powered distribution step. Current primary sources conflict in ways that matter:

- EGO HT2600/HT2600-FC says to use a soft brush, use resin solvent for pitch or sticky residue, never use water to clean the trimmer, and lubricate the blades with lightweight machine oil before and after each use.
- Makita XHU09 says to remove dust with a wire brush, wipe with a cloth, not wash the shear blades in water, and use low-viscosity machine oil or spray-type lubricating oil before operation, once per hour during operation, and after operation.
- STIHL HSA 150 R/T says to remove the battery and wear heavy-duty gloves, use resin solvent on both sides, briefly run the blades to distribute it, and not pressure-wash or spray the cutting blades with water or other liquids.
- BLACK+DECKER's US support page says soap and water may be used on hedge-trimmer blades if they are dried and lubricated before storage; it also says brushing followed by WD-40 or a similar spray lubricant is sufficient.
- Husqvarna's support guidance says to disconnect the spark plug or remove the battery, wear gloves, clear debris, wipe the blades with a damp cloth, grease with a light oil, clean before and after every use, and lubricate before long storage.

The contradiction is the central editorial opportunity. WhoAdvice should give a safe common sequence—identify the model, isolate its power source, protect hands, remove loose debris, follow the exact manual for liquids and chemicals, dry where applicable, apply the specified lubricant, inspect, and store—then make model-specific instructions authoritative.

## Common sequence supported across sources

The sources support the following high-level order, with all chemical and operating details deferred to the exact manual:

1. Identify the exact model and read its maintenance section.
2. Stop the machine, wait for all blade movement to cease, and isolate its power source as the manual directs.
3. Wear the blade-handling protection required by the manual; heavy work gloves are explicitly required in STIHL and Husqvarna guidance.
4. Place the tool securely and keep hands away from cutting edges.
5. Remove loose leaves, twigs, dirt, and dust with the permitted brush or cloth method.
6. Treat pitch, sap, or resin only with a cleaner the exact manual permits, keeping liquids and solvents away from incompatible plastic, electrical, fuel, and drive components.
7. Dry the blade when the approved method introduces moisture.
8. Inspect for looseness, cracks, bent or damaged teeth, abnormal alignment, or persistent binding.
9. Apply the exact specified blade lubricant at the stated frequency. Do not substitute gearbox grease, fuel-mix oil, bar-and-chain oil, vegetable oil, or a household spray without model-specific support.
10. Follow the exact manual for any brief powered distribution; otherwise do not reconnect power during cleaning.
11. Remove excess product where directed, fit the scabbard or blade cover, and store dry and out of children's reach.

This is an editorial synthesis, not a universal procedure. The article must visibly tell readers to follow their exact manual where its instructions differ.

## Chemical and material-safety findings

### Resin solvent is not a generic chemical category

The STIHL US blade cleaner/resin remover Safety Data Sheet identifies a specific aerosol product and was revised April 3, 2024. It requires users to read precautions, use personal protective equipment as required, store it locked and in a well-ventilated place, protect it from sunlight and temperatures above 122°F, avoid breathing vapors during a spill, ventilate, and eliminate ignition sources. These findings support a rule to read the exact product label and SDS and to keep aerosol cleaner away from heat, sparks, flames, and poorly ventilated storage.

They do not prove that every resin solvent has the same formulation or hazard profile.

### Plastic housings need separate treatment

EGO warns that commercial solvents may damage plastics. STIHL says not to use detergents or solvents on polymer components. Makita warns against gasoline, benzine, thinner, alcohol, and similar chemicals because they may cause discoloration, deformation, or cracks. The article must separate blade-surface cleaning from housing, battery, charger, socket, motor, gearbox, and fuel-system care.

### Water instructions conflict

- EGO: never use water for cleaning the trimmer.
- STIHL HSA 150: do not spray the cutting blades with water or other liquids.
- Makita XHU09: do not wash shear blades in water.
- BLACK+DECKER brand support: soap and water may be used on blades if they are dried and lubricated.
- Husqvarna support: a damp cloth is permitted.

Therefore, the public article must not answer `Can you wash hedge-trimmer blades with water?` with a universal yes or no. The direct answer should be: only if the exact manual permits the specific method, and never immerse or flood the tool.

## Lubricant findings

- EGO specifies lightweight machine oil before and after each use.
- Makita specifies low-viscosity machine oil or spray-type lubricating oil before use, once per hour during use, and after use.
- Husqvarna describes a light oil and long-storage lubrication.
- STIHL uses a resin remover and lubricant on the blades and separately specifies gearbox grease for the gearbox.
- BLACK+DECKER's short US support article permits WD-40 or a similar spray-type lubricant before storage.

These primary sources contradict broad online claims that WD-40 is always appropriate or never appropriate. The article should avoid adjudicating the chemistry in the abstract and instead instruct the reader to match the exact manual. It should also explain that `hedge trimmer oil` may refer to at least four different things—blade lubricant, resin-remover/lubricant spray, gearbox grease, or gas-engine two-cycle oil—and these are not interchangeable.

## Cleaning frequency findings

Frequency varies by source and condition:

- EGO: blade lubrication before and after each use.
- Makita XHU09: lubrication before use, once per hour during use, and after use.
- Husqvarna support: clean before and after every use and lubricate before long storage.
- STIHL HSA 150 maintenance chart provides example intervals and says actual use and experience affect inspection and maintenance frequency.

The article can recommend checking the exact manual and cleaning promptly when debris or sticky residue is present. It should not present one universal hourly or calendar schedule.

## Damage, binding, and stop-work rules

Cleaning is not enough when:

- blades are cracked, bent, warped, loose, missing teeth, or otherwise damaged;
- controls or safety devices do not operate correctly;
- blade movement remains abnormal after permitted cleaning and lubrication;
- the tool has been exposed to liquid contrary to its manual;
- a recalled model/serial range is involved;
- the owner would need to dismantle components beyond the manual's owner-maintenance scope.

STIHL HSA 150 tells users to replace damaged cutting blades before use and routes worn or damaged blades to an authorized dealer. EGO routes repairs to a qualified service technician. Makita routes repairs and non-described adjustment to authorized or factory service centers.

## Recall and safety check

This page does not recommend models, but the article should include a concise pre-maintenance recall check because unexpected blade activation has driven serious hedge-trimmer recalls:

- CPSC recall 25-334, June 12, 2025: specified RYOBI 40V 24-inch models and serial ranges could activate after only one control was pressed; 27 reports included 16 laceration injuries.
- CPSC recall 23-212: EGO HT2410 units from specified manufacturing dates could activate unexpectedly because the rear trigger might not rebound.
- CPSC recall 22-197: specified Makita XHU07/XHU08 model and serial ranges lacked the required teal blade guard.
- CPSC recall 16-195: STIHL HSA 65 could operate unexpectedly or continue running.

None of those records applies to the EGO HT2600, Makita XHU09, or STIHL HSA 150 manuals used as cleaning examples based on the model identifiers in the recall notices. The article must not describe those examples as `recall-free`; it should advise owners to check their exact model and serial number against current CPSC and manufacturer notices before maintenance or use.

2. Use one common workflow with visible `check your manual` branches for water, resin solvent, lubricant, frequency, and powered distribution.
3. Include a compact manual comparison table using EGO HT2600, STIHL HSA 150, Makita XHU09, BLACK+DECKER brand support, and Husqvarna support as examples—not recommendations.

6. Put a safety callout before any step that reconnects power: only do so when the exact manual explicitly instructs it, keep hands and materials clear, and disconnect again before further handling.
7. Include a short recall check and service boundary rather than implying cleaning fixes mechanical or control faults.

9. Do not include affiliate cleaning products, universal household substitutes, homemade solvent recipes, or disinfection claims in the initial article contract.
