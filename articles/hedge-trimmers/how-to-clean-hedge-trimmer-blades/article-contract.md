## Binding safety and decision sequence

Present this order near the beginning and preserve it throughout the article:

1. Identify the exact model and open its maintenance instructions.
2. Stop the tool, wait for blade movement to cease, and isolate its power source as directed.
3. Use the blade-handling protection and stable work position required by the manual.
4. Remove loose debris with a permitted brush or cloth method.
5. Treat sap or resin only with a cleaner and application method permitted for that model.
6. Dry the blade if the approved method introduces moisture.
7. Inspect the blade, fasteners, guard, and cover before reconnecting power.
8. Apply the specified blade lubricant at the stated frequency.
9. Reconnect power for distribution only when the exact manual explicitly requires it, then isolate power again before further handling.
10. Fit the cover or scabbard and store the clean, dry tool as its manual directs.

This is a manual-first decision sequence, not a universal chemical or mechanical procedure.

## Cleaning procedure contract

### 1. Identify the model and isolate power

- Tell the reader to locate the model identifier and maintenance section first.
- Cover battery removal, unplugging, or gas-model disabling only at category level; the exact manual supplies the action.
- Wait for all movement to stop before handling the tool.
- Do not imply that an off switch alone isolates every trimmer.

### 2. Stabilize the tool and protect hands

- Use the manual's required gloves and position the tool so it cannot slip or rotate.
- Keep hands and loose cleaning material away from tooth edges and pinch points.
- Do not imply gloves make a connected tool safe.

### 3. Remove loose debris

- Describe brushing or wiping only as the broad task.
- Tell the reader to use the brush or cloth type permitted by the manual.
- Do not universally approve wire brushes, compressed air, or water.

### 4. Remove sap or resin

- Apply only a manual-permitted cleaner in the directed amount and location.
- Keep cleaner away from plastics, vents, electrical parts, batteries, chargers, hot engine parts, fuel-system components, and the gearbox unless their instructions explicitly allow contact.
- When an aerosol example is discussed, lead with label/SDS ventilation, ignition, heat, and PPE controls.
- Do not claim all products sold as resin solvent share one formulation or compatibility profile.

### 5. Dry and inspect

- Require thorough drying when the approved method introduces moisture.
- Inspect teeth, blade alignment, fasteners, guard, and cover for cracks, bending, looseness, corrosion, or abnormal movement.
- Do not tell readers to adjust fasteners, remove blades, abrade corrosion, or correct alignment without exact-model instructions.

### 6. Lubricate

- Use the exact lubricant type, application point, amount, and schedule from the manual.
- Explain that blade lubricant is not automatically interchangeable with gearbox grease, two-cycle oil, bar-and-chain oil, penetrating oil, or a household spray.
- Remove excess only where the instructions require it.

### 7. Distribute only when explicitly directed

- Put a second safety callout before any discussion of briefly running a blade.
- Attribute the example to the exact manual; do not turn it into a general step.
- Require the area to be clear of hands, cloths, tools, and other materials.
- Require power isolation again before any further blade handling.

### 8. Cover and store

- Fit the cover or scabbard and store the tool dry, secure, and out of children's reach.
- Keep aerosol or chemical storage instructions tied to the exact product label/SDS.
- Keep battery, fuel, engine, charger, and gearbox storage details out of scope.
