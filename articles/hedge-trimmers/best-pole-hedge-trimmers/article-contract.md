## Approved buying-guide structure

Keep this section pre-purchase only and draft in this order:

### Measure the hedge and separate tool length from claimed reach

Compare hedge height and face depth with actual tool length, collapsed/assembled length, and qualified user-inclusive reach. Explain storage and lower-face implications.

### Check balance and weight distribution, not weight alone

Explain leverage and why a nominally lighter tool may still feel difficult when mass is concentrated near the cutting head. Do not claim untested models are well balanced.

### Choose fixed, sectional, or telescopic reach

Compare rigidity, storage, setup, adjustment range, possible flex, and full-extension control.

### Inspect the head angle and locking system

Compare useful angle range, position count, adjustment method, and documented lock design. Position count alone does not prove easy or secure adjustment.

### Match blade length and stated capacity to maintained growth

Explain coverage versus maneuverability. Treat tooth gap and stated capacity as selection limits, not independent proof of repeated cutting performance.

### Calculate the complete working package

Compare the additional battery and charger purchases required for WORX WG252.9 and BLACK+DECKER LPHT120B without hard-coding prices. Compare package completeness, compatible batteries already owned, confirmed straps or sheaths, chargers, and replacement support.

### Understand why no corded model made the shortlist

Explain that corded candidates were considered, but none met the current exact-model availability, support, package, and affiliate-eligibility requirements used for this shortlist. Corded power may still suit a small property with reliable outdoor-outlet access and a manageable cable route.

### Decide whether gas ownership is justified

Reserve gas for readers whose repeated workload supports the extra weight, fuel mixing, starting, noise, exhaust, and maintenance.

### Check warranty, manuals, parts, and storage needs
