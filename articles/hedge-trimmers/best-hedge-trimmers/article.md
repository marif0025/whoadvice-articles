The best hedge trimmer for your garden depends on three practical details: how much hedge you have, how high it grows, and where you can get power. A long blade saves passes on a broad boundary, but it can feel awkward around a small shrub. Weight matters just as much as blade size. Check whether the listed figure includes the battery, then think about how long you will hold the tool at chest or shoulder height. For cordless models, include the battery and charger in your budget rather than comparing bare-tool prices with complete kits.

## Which type of hedge trimmer do you need?

Hedge trimmers come in four main types. Choose by the distance from a power source, the height of the hedge, and how long you expect each trimming session to last.

If your choice has narrowed to mains or battery power, our [corded vs cordless hedge trimmer comparison](/garden-tools/corded-vs-cordless-hedge-trimmer/) applies the outlet-route, runtime, weight, and full-cost tests in more detail.

### Cordless trimmers

Cordless hedge trimmers let you move between boundaries, gates, and separate planting areas without managing a cable. They suit gardens where an outlet is too far away or the route is awkward. The fitted battery adds weight, limits working time, and may raise the initial cost. If cable-free movement fits your garden, [compare the best cordless hedge trimmers](/garden-tools/best-cordless-hedge-trimmers/) by loaded weight, battery package, and cutting capacity.

### Corded trimmers

Corded hedge trimmers provide steady power without battery charging. They suit compact gardens where an outdoor outlet is close and the lead can follow a clear route. The cable limits movement and must remain behind the cutting path. If that setup suits your garden, compare more options in our [guide to the best corded hedge trimmers](/garden-tools/best-corded-hedge-trimmers/).

### Gas trimmers

Gas hedge trimmers can support long, frequent sessions without waiting for a battery to recharge. They suit demanding work where fuel storage and engine upkeep are acceptable. The tradeoffs are more noise, exhaust, maintenance, and starting effort than an electric tool.

### Pole trimmers

Pole hedge trimmers place the blade above the reach of a standard model. They suit tall faces and hedge tops that can be seen and controlled from the ground. The long shaft increases leverage on your arms and makes close, detailed shaping harder. Compare exact reach and operating weight in our [pole hedge trimmer guide](/garden-tools/best-pole-hedge-trimmers/).

## How to choose a hedge trimmer

Measure the hedge before comparing tools. Its length, height, width, and stem condition tell you more than voltage or motor size.

### Measure the work you need to cover

A few shaped shrubs need control more than blade length. A long boundary rewards a wider cutting sweep, while a tall hedge may require a pole head. Note whether you trim soft new growth often or leave stems to become thicker and woodier.

### Set a budget for a working tool

Compare the cost of everything required on the first day. That may include a battery, charger, outdoor extension lead, fuel, shoulder strap, or blade sheath. A low bare-tool price is misleading when essential parts cost extra.

### Check whether a cord can reach cleanly

Choose corded only when the lead can reach every hedge without crossing busy paths, wrapping around obstacles, or running close to the blade. Cordless costs more upfront but is easier around gates, sheds, corners, and separate planting areas.

### Choose blade length and capacity separately

Blade length controls how much hedge you cover in one pass. Cut capacity describes the stated opening between the teeth. A long blade helps on broad, flat faces; a shorter one is easier around compact shrubs. Our [hedge trimmer sizing guide](/garden-tools/what-size-hedge-trimmer-do-i-need/) explains the difference in detail.

### Compare the handle at your usual cutting angle

A fixed handle is adequate for simple waist-height work. A rotating rear handle is more useful when you switch between tops and vertical sides. For a pole model, compare head angles and complete working weight because extra reach also increases leverage on your arms.

### Check what the cordless package includes

Confirm the exact battery platform, capacity, charger, and package suffix. A bare tool can be economical when you already own compatible batteries. New platform buyers should compare its full setup cost with one of the [cordless hedge-trimmer kits](/garden-tools/best-cordless-hedge-trimmers/) that works out of the box.

## Hedge trimmer questions gardeners ask

These are the practical questions that often remain after comparing blade length, power, and package contents.

### Is a cordless hedge trimmer worth it for a small yard?

Yes, when a cable would be awkward to route around the garden. For a short hedge beside an outlet, the BLACK+DECKER BEHT350FF avoids battery cost and charging. Cordless becomes more useful when the lead would cross paths, catch on corners, or fail to reach separate hedge areas.

### Should I stay with the battery brand I already own?

Stay with your current battery platform when its hedge trimmer fits the job. The DEWALT DCHT821B makes sense for owners who already have compatible 20V MAX batteries and a charger. A familiar battery is not a good reason to accept the wrong blade length, weight, or handle.

### Does higher voltage mean better cutting?

No. Voltage identifies the power system, but it does not tell you how well a trimmer matches your hedge. Compare blade length, stated cut capacity, complete weight, handle design, and the supplied battery instead.

### Do I need a pole trimmer for a 10- or 12-foot hedge?

You need a pole trimmer only when the upper face or top is beyond safe reach with a standard model. The DEWALT DCPH820M1 offers an articulating head, but its 12-foot maximum-reach claim includes the user’s height. Make sure you can still see and control the blade from the ground.

### Can a hedge trimmer cut a 1-inch branch?

Only attempt it with a model that states a 1-inch capacity, and remember that the branch may still be too hard or woody. The capacity figure describes the blade opening, not a guaranteed clean cut. Use our [hedge trimmer, lopper, and pruning saw comparison](/garden-tools/hedge-trimmer-vs-loppers-vs-pruning-saw/) if the blade stalls or the branch bends.

### How can I keep the extension cord away from the blade?

Plan the cable route before cutting and keep the lead behind you. A cord-retention feature, such as the one on the BLACK+DECKER BEHT350FF, helps prevent unplugging but does not protect the cable from the blade. Use an outdoor-rated lead of the gauge specified in the manual, with the [CPSC extension-cord guidance](https://www.cpsc.gov/FAQ/Extension-Cords?language=en) providing additional hazard context.
