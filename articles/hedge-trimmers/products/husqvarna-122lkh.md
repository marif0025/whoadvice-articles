### Independent observations

- Bob Vila tested the exact model and reported clean cutting up to roughly 0.75-inch material in its test.
- With the grips at chest height, its tester trimmed vertically to about 10 ft and horizontally at about 8 ft; those are tester-specific observations, not universal reach claims.
- The supplied shoulder strap reportedly reduced strain in that test, but the tool remains the heaviest shortlist entry and requires fuel, starting, and engine maintenance.

### Safety and recall

- Official manual covers fuel mixing, hot surfaces, exhaust, blade transport, PPE, bystanders, and maintenance.
- No exact 122LKH recall was located on July 15, 2026. CPSC results include recalls for different Husqvarna models: 225H, 122HD45/122HD60, and several grass trimmers. Do not generalize those recalls to 122LKH; recheck before publication.

### Normalized interpretation

- Best fit: larger or repeated tall-hedge workloads where a gas powerhead and attachment system justify the weight and maintenance.
- Decision value: independent cutting and reach observations plus a serviceable multi-attachment platform.
- Main tradeoffs: 14.96-lb weight, fuel mixing, noise, exhaust, starting procedure, and a much higher ownership burden than cordless homeowner tools.
