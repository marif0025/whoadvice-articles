## Reader decision

The reader must decide whether the planned work is routine surface trimming within controlled reach or a different job involving thick woody stems, unstable access, overhead hazards, utilities, nests, or major restorative pruning. The article should establish a stop rule before technique: use the correct tool, follow the exact manual, and isolate power before touching the blade or clearing a jam.

## Page role

- Own the operating sequence through shutdown, power isolation, visual inspection, sheathing, transport, and the storage transition.
- Provide power-format branches for corded, cordless, gas, and pole tools without ranking them.
- Route blade size, product selection, thick branches, resin removal, lubrication, blade cleaning, and sharpening to their canonical pages.
- Avoid becoming a horticultural hedge-pruning calendar or plant-specific pruning guide.

## Opening angle

Begin with the work-area and tool-fit check. Before starting, identify people, pets, hidden wire or fencing, utility hazards, unstable footing, thick stems, wildlife or nests, and the safe route for any cord. If the task cannot be controlled with both hands and stable footing within the tool's manual, the technique section does not apply.

## Required pre-use decision table

| Check | Continue only when | Route elsewhere when |
|---|---|---|
| Stem condition | Growth fits the exact tool's intended routine trimming job | Thick woody stems need loppers, pruning saw, or professional assessment |
| Reach and footing | Both hands, stable footing, visibility, and clearance are maintained | A ladder, overreach, utility proximity, or unstable ground is involved |
| Work area | People, pets, hard obstacles, wire, and cord route are controlled | Hidden hazards or public access cannot be isolated |
| Wildlife | Current regional guidance permits work and no active nest or protected wildlife issue is present | Work may disturb nesting birds or protected wildlife |
| Tool condition | Blade, guard, controls, fasteners, power source, and sheath pass the manual's checks | Damage, looseness, leakage, abnormal movement, or a safety-device fault is found |

## Operating boundaries

- Do not publish one universal grip, stance, cutting direction, ladder rule, wet-weather rule, PPE list, start procedure, or jam-clearing process without reconciling exact manuals and safety authorities.
- Never instruct the reader to hold cut material, clear debris, inspect teeth, or touch the blade while a power source remains connected or the blade can move.
- Do not equate a manufacturer's maximum stated cut capacity with a safe routine branch size.
- Do not suggest that a pole hedge trimmer replaces a pole saw or makes all elevated work safe.
- Do not give gas fueling, battery repair, extension-cord sizing, or engine-service instructions beyond the approved evidence and page scope.
- Do not provide plant-specific pruning timing without authoritative regional horticultural support.

## Evidence requirements

- Research representative corded, cordless, gas, and pole manuals and preserve format-specific differences.
- Use current CPSC or equivalent US consumer-safety guidance for powered garden equipment and recall awareness.
- Use authoritative electrical guidance for outdoor cords, GFCI protection, damaged leads, and wet conditions where applicable.
- Use extension or horticultural sources for plant health, formal hedge technique, and wildlife timing; identify regional limits.
- Verify PPE, two-handed control, transport, jam clearing, blade coast-down, and storage instructions instead of relying on generic tutorials.
- Do not imply WhoAdvice performed or observed the technique.
