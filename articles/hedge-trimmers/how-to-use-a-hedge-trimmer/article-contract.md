### 3. Check the manual, recall status, and tool condition

### 5. Prepare and operate the power source correctly

Use short H3 branches in this order:

1. **Corded hedge trimmers:** Outdoor-rated cord, exact manual's gauge/length rules, GFCI-protected supply where required, inspected plugs/leads, and cord routed behind and away from the cutter. A damaged cord is a stop-work event.
2. **Cordless hedge trimmers:** Inspect battery and contacts, install only at the manual's point in the sequence, and remove the battery before clearing, inspection, transport, cleaning, storage, or leaving the tool unattended where the manual requires it.
3. **Gas hedge trimmers:** Keep the explanation manual-led and concise. Cover outdoor ventilation, cool-engine fueling, hot components after shutdown, and blade-stop confirmation. Do not publish a universal choke, primer, mixture, spark-plug, or service procedure.

Do not turn this section into a corded-versus-cordless buying comparison.

### 6. Use stable control and a measured cutting sequence

- Require two hands in the positions specified by the exact manual, dry and clear handles, stable ground, visibility, and the tool in front of the operator.
- Use a supported sequence: remove incompatible thick stems with the correct tool, trim the sides before the top, work upward on the sides, and make small controlled passes.
- Describe a base slightly wider than the top as horticultural shaping guidance.
- Omit the STIHL 0–10° value and all universal numeric angles.
- Do not describe a maximum cutting gap as a safe routine branch size.

### 7. Stop, wait, isolate, and deal with a jam

- Warning order must remain: release the controls; keep hands and objects away; wait for complete blade stop; isolate the power source using the exact format-specific procedure; then follow the manual's clearing or inspection instructions.
- Format-specific isolation: unplug a corded model or remove a battery where the manual requires it. For a gas model, stop the engine, wait for complete blade stop, account for hot components, and follow the exact manual's required anti-restart or maintenance-isolation step before clearing or inspection.
- Never instruct the reader to pull held material, pry the teeth, touch the blade, restart against the blockage, or clear debris while the blade can move.
- Treat repeated stalling as a tool/material mismatch, damage, or service signal rather than a reason to force the cut.

### 8. Stop for reach, weather, fatigue, wildlife, or utility hazards

- Pole trimmers are for routine fine growth within the exact model's controlled ground-level reach. They do not replace a pole saw or authorize unstable access.
- The representative manuals reviewed prohibit ladder use; tell readers to follow their exact manual and use a qualified professional when secure ground-level control is not possible.
- Omit DEWALT's 10 ft figure. The public utility rule is nonnumeric: stop work near overhead or hidden electrical conductors and consult current utility/local guidance or a qualified professional.
- For rain or wet vegetation, follow the exact manual. A GFCI or ingress rating does not override a manual prohibition; postpone when permission or footing is uncertain.
- Stop before fatigue or heat undermines grip, balance, visibility, or control. Keep noise only in the manual-specific hearing-protection context.
- Wildlife wording: `Stop if you find an active or uncertain nest. Leave the area undisturbed and check current U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service and state wildlife guidance before resuming work.`

### 9. Transport, store, and move to the right maintenance guide

- After shutdown and isolation, follow the dedicated cleaning procedure when cleaning is needed. Fit the sheath or scabbard before carrying, transporting, or storing the clean tool, as the exact manual directs.
- Complete the transition with a visual damage check, safe carrying orientation, vehicle restraint, and dry secure storage.
