## Reader decision

The reader must choose two different dimensions: blade length determines coverage and maneuverability, while tooth gap or stated cut capacity describes the opening available for stems. The article must stop readers from assuming that a longer blade cuts thicker wood or that a published maximum guarantees a clean cut through every branch.

## Opening angle

Begin with the two measurements shoppers confuse. The hedge's face width and the amount of detailed shaping influence blade length; the thicker woody stems inside the hedge influence whether a hedge trimmer is suitable at all. Height is a separate reach question and may require a pole model rather than simply a longer blade.

## Key explanations

- Blade length affects coverage, maneuverability, balance, and the number of passes.
- Tooth spacing and a manufacturer's stated cut capacity are related specifications but should not be treated as identical without exact-model documentation.
- Maximum capacity is not a promise of equal performance across green, dry, soft, or hard wood.
- A long blade does not replace a pole shaft for height.
- Tool-only weight can mislead cordless shoppers; use loaded weight when it is officially published or transparently calculated from compatible official components.
