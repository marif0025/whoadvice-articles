## Reader decision

The reader must first determine whether the blade is dull, resin-coated, misaligned, bent, chipped, cracked, or otherwise damaged. Sharpening is not the correct response to every cutting problem. The article should help the reader stop at the right boundary: clean the blade, follow an exact manual's sharpening method, use a qualified service, or replace the blade or tool.

## Opening angle

Lead with diagnosis and power isolation. A dirty blade may need cleaning rather than metal removal, while a bent, cracked, badly chipped, loose, or misaligned blade may require service or replacement. Before touching the blade, the owner must follow the exact manual for disconnecting the plug, removing the battery, or disabling the engine and ignition system.

## Method boundaries

- Do not publish one universal bevel angle, file size, stroke count, grinder speed, tooth side, or blade-removal procedure.
- Do not assume a Dremel or rotary tool is approved merely because third-party tutorials demonstrate it.
- Preserve manufacturer geometry; do not claim that changing an angle improves performance.
- Treat heat discoloration, excessive material removal, powered-tool kick, and blade movement as risks requiring sourced controls.
- Do not tell readers to bypass guards, switches, interlocks, battery terminals, spark-plug precautions, or manufacturer service limits.
- Do not imply that sharpening repairs bent, cracked, loose, or misaligned blades.
