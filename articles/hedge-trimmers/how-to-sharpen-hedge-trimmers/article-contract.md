## Binding decision and safety sequence

Preserve this order throughout the article:

1. Identify the exact model and locate its current maintenance instructions.
2. Check the exact model and serial number for an unresolved recall or manufacturer safety notice.
3. Stop the tool, wait for all blade movement to cease, and isolate its power source exactly as directed.
4. Clean and inspect the blade before deciding to remove metal.
5. Route dirt or resin to cleaning; route damage, looseness, misalignment, unclear geometry, or unsupported access to service or replacement.
6. When the current exact-model manual permits owner sharpening, use its named hand file, cutting surfaces, angle, access method, and service limits.
7. Never reconnect, briefly run, pry, or remove the blade merely to expose hidden edges unless the exact manual provides the complete procedure.
8. Remove burrs and filings, apply the specified lubricant, restore fixed guards and owner-removable components, and complete only the manual-approved check. Refit the blade cover or scabbard after any permitted operational check and before storage or transport.
9. Stop if blade movement remains abnormal, controls do not work correctly, or sharpening would require work outside the owner's maintenance scope.

This is a manual-first decision guide, not a universal sharpening recipe.

## Hand-file procedure contract

Use numbered steps. Each step must remain conditional on the exact manual.

### 1. Confirm owner sharpening is permitted

- Verify exact model, manual revision, named file or stone, angle or geometry, cutting surfaces, access method, and service boundary.
- If the manual gives no owner procedure or the instructions cannot be matched confidently to the tool, stop and use professional service.

### 2. Isolate power and stabilize the tool

- Require complete blade stop and exact-manual power isolation.
- Position and secure the tool only as its manual permits.
- Keep the cutting assembly installed unless the exact manual expressly permits owner removal.

### 3. Clean and inspect before filing

- Route substantial sap, resin, or debris to the cleaning guide.
- Recheck for bending, cracks, deep chips, looseness, misalignment, or abnormal blade movement after cleaning.
- Stop if damage or access uncertainty remains.

### 4. Identify only the permitted cutting edges

- Use the exact manual's diagram or description.
- Do not infer that both sides of every tooth, a guard, a blunt projection, or a flat sliding face should be filed.
- If the cutting edge cannot be identified confidently, stop.

### 5. Match the exact angle or existing geometry

- Use the exact model's stated angle when supplied.
- When the manual says to follow the original cutting-edge shape, preserve that shape without publishing a substitute number.
- Do not advise changing the angle for a sharper, faster, or longer-lasting edge.

### 6. File only in the approved direction

- A forward-only stroke may be described where supported by the manual example.
- Lift the file on the return stroke when the exact procedure directs it.
- Remove as little material as needed; do not prescribe a universal pass count.
- Keep treatment consistent without claiming that identical stroke counts guarantee correct geometry.

### 7. Stop at covered or inaccessible edges

Place this warning inside the procedure:

> Never reconnect, briefly run, or pry the blades merely to expose covered cutting edges unless the exact manual provides that procedure.

- Do not publish a general blade-offset, powered repositioning, screwdriver, wood-block, or prying method.
- If the manual's access method is absent, unclear, or outside owner maintenance, route to professional service.

### 8. Repeat only on the surfaces the manual identifies

- Do not say `sharpen both sides of every tooth` universally.
- Do not instruct blade removal or reassembly as a routine step.
- Stop if the tool shifts, the file contacts a guard or non-cutting surface, or the geometry cannot be maintained.
