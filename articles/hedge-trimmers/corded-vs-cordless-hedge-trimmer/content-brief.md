## Reader decision

The reader needs a defensible format choice before comparing individual models. The article should turn the garden layout into a decision: whether every hedge has a safe, unobstructed route to an outdoor outlet; whether the reader already owns a compatible battery system; and whether continuous power matters more than cable-free movement.

## Page role

- Own the format-comparison intent between the mixed-format hub and the two power-specific commercial branches.
- Expand the short corded and cordless descriptions in the main roundup without repeating its ranked products.
- Send readers who have chosen a format to the corresponding buying guide.
- Avoid becoming another general `best hedge trimmer` page.

## Opening angle

Start with the outlet-and-route test. A corded trimmer is viable only when a correctly rated lead can reach every hedge without creating an impractical route. Cordless is easier across gates, corners, paths, and separated planting areas, but the reader must account for battery weight, working time, charger availability, and full kit cost.

## Decision rules to develop

- A short hedge beside a suitable outlet generally favors corded value when the cable has a clean route.
- Multiple hedge areas, gates, corners, or distant boundaries generally favor cordless movement.
- Existing compatible batteries can change cordless economics, but only when the available trimmer also fits the hedge.
- Long sessions do not automatically favor either format: corded needs a manageable cable route, while cordless needs verified battery capacity or a second compatible battery.
- Voltage alone must never be used to declare a cordless model more capable.

## Evidence requirements

- Use exact-model manuals to support extension-cord gauge, electrical, battery, charging, and operating warnings.
- Attribute runtime and cutting-capacity claims to manufacturers unless independent evidence is available.
- Distinguish bare-tool, kit, and loaded weights.
- Treat prices as dated observations rather than evergreen facts.
- Check current CPSC recalls before naming any model as an example.
- Do not imply that WhoAdvice compared tools through hands-on use.
