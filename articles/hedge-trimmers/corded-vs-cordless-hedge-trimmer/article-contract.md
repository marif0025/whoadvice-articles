Immediately after the introduction, add a compact decision box with two parallel lists:

```md
Choose corded if:
- Every hedge is near a suitable outlet.
- The cable route is simple.
- You want continuous mains access while connected.

Choose cordless if:

- Gates, paths, or obstacles interrupt the route.
- Cable-free movement matters more than avoiding charging.
```

The box is an answer summary, not a product recommendation. Keep each item conditional and do not introduce claims that are absent from the body.

## Corded-format section contract

Lead with the qualifying condition: every hedge must be reachable with a permitted outdoor extension cord by a practical route.

Cover:

- Nearby hedges and an unobstructed route
- Continuous mains access while properly connected, without calling it unlimited operation
- The cord-management burden test: hedge sides, gates, driveways/walkways, trees, furniture, raised beds, long boundaries, and elevated work
- State explicitly that a corded tool becomes significantly less practical from a ladder because the operator must manage both footing and cable position; do not turn this into general ladder-use instruction
- The separately purchased outdoor extension cord as part of the complete setup
- Exact-manual control over cord type, gauge, length, connection, cord retention, and any GFCI instruction
- The main tradeoff: the operator must manage the cord throughout the job

End with a contextual transition to the corded buying guide. CMS owns the live internal link.

## Cordless-format section contract

Lead with the qualifying condition: movement across separated, distant, or obstructed hedge areas matters more than avoiding battery planning.

Cover:

- Gates, corners, paths, distant boundaries, and multiple planting areas
- Included battery/charger versus bare-tool packages

## Cost comparison contract

Compare complete first-purchase and ownership configurations:

- Corded tool + exact-manual-compliant outdoor extension cord
- Cordless kit with the required battery and charger
- Cordless bare tool only for a reader with an expressly approved compatible battery and charger
- Possible second/replacement battery versus replacement of a damaged cord

Do not state that corded is always cheaper. Do not use current dollar prices unless both complete setups are refreshed on the same date immediately before drafting and the date is visible. Omit lifetime electricity calculations.

## Working-session contract

- Separate runtime from recharge downtime.
- For cordless, cover the exact included battery/charger combination, removable-battery status, whether a faster charger is included or optional, and how many approved packs would be required to avoid a gap.
- Attribute manufacturer runtime and recharge claims beside the number and preserve stated conditions.
- Do not publish a universal runtime range or use the conflicting WORX WG261 runtime answers.
- For corded, describe continuous mains access while correctly connected; do not imply limitless working time, superior cutting, or exemption from route constraints.

## Weight, balance, and control contract

- Compare complete operating configurations: corded tool weight versus cordless tool with the selected battery installed.
- Keep bare, manufacturer-listed operating, and calculated weights explicitly labeled.
- Explain that weight does not establish balance or fatigue.
- Where evidence permits, compare battery placement, blade length, rotating rear handle, handle reach/grip positions, and control on vertical faces versus horizontal tops.
- Do not infer balance from specifications or claim that either format is categorically lighter.

## Cutting-performance contract

- Answer directly that capable tools exist in both formats, so the power source alone does not decide cutting performance.
- Consumer Reports may support only the narrow statement that battery models matched corded models in its specific testing; do not generalize this to all tools.
- Do not compare cordless voltage with corded amperage as equivalent measures or use voltage alone as a power ranking.
- Treat blade length, strokes per minute, tooth gap, and stated capacity as exact-model fields rather than proof of real-world performance.
- If routine stems are woody, dead, mature, or near a model's stated maximum, direct the reader to a more suitable exact model or the lopper/pruning-saw guide rather than promising either format will cope.
- Noise receives at most one short paragraph: both formats avoid engine noise, but there is no approved format winner without comparable measured exact-model data.

## Safety section contract

Lead with the action: follow the exact tool and power-system manuals; format does not override model-specific restrictions.

Cover only decision-relevant differences:

- Corded: outdoor-rated permitted cord, inspection, routing clear of blades, connection/retention, and exact-manual electrical instructions
- Cordless: remove/disconnect the battery before jam clearing, servicing, or cleaning; use the approved charger and battery instructions
- Weather: IP ratings are model-specific and do not automatically permit trimming in rain; tool, battery, charger, and cord connection may have different restrictions
- Both: blade-stop, two-hand, transport, storage, PPE, and pre-use inspection instructions come from the exact manual

Do not reproduce a universal extension-cord gauge table. Do not call any model safe, rain-safe, or recall-free. Recall details belong only if they materially clarify a named representative model and have been refreshed immediately before drafting.
