## Buying-guide contract

Use `## How to choose the best epilator`. Keep every subsection focused on checks made before purchase.

1. **Treatment Area** — confirm manufacturer-approved areas and whether a dedicated facial device is required.
2. **Epilator Type** — choose the root-plucking mechanism and format that match the intended routine.
3. **Wet/Dry and Power** — compare shower use, cordless freedom, and continuous corded power.
4. **Head Design** — compare fixed, pivoting, fully flexible, wide, and precision heads.
5. **Speed and Comfort Controls** — cover speed settings, massage caps, pressure-responsive controls, lights, and guidance attachments without implying every model has pressure detection.
6. **Battery and Charging** — check runtime, charge time, plug restrictions, and replacement-battery needs.
7. **Attachments** — count only extras that replace a tool or materially change the intended use.
8. **Cleaning and Support** — check washable parts, cleaning instructions, warranty, replacement heads, and service support.
9. **Safety** — check intended areas, wet-use permission, damaged-skin restrictions, and manual warnings before purchase.
10. **Epilator vs. IPL** — complete the approved category-comparison group by distinguishing mechanical epilation from IPL, shaving, and waxing.

Avoid duplicating the type section: the type section explains classifications; the buying guide gives purchase rules.
