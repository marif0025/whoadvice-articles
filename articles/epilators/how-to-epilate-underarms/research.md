## Research decision

Proceed with a dedicated, non-commercial underarm-epilation guide. The article should not publish one device-independent routine. The safe editorial pattern is:

1. Confirm that the manufacturer permits the exact epilator head and attachment for underarm epilation.
2. Check the exact instructions for its power and wet/dry limits.
3. Follow the exact model's hair-length, angle, cap, speed, and cleaning instructions.
4. Stretch the underarm skin and use light pressure, slow movement, and controlled passes.
5. Stop if the skin is cut or bleeding, or if pain is unusual, severe, or worsening; do not describe bleeding as a normal milestone.

## Proposed step sequence for approval

This sequence is a synthesis of current evidence. Every step that varies by device must visibly branch to the exact manual.

1. **Check the exact model, head, and cap.** Confirm that the manufacturer permits epilation—not merely shaving or trimming—on underarms. Do not infer permitted use from the brand or series name.
2. **Check when the manual says not to use the device.** The Philips 2026 manual says not to use the product on skin that is damaged, inflamed, irritated, healing, or affected by a skin condition. It also identifies health situations that require a doctor's advice. Public wording should closely paraphrase the manual and avoid diagnosing the reader.
3. **Inspect and clean the head before use.** Stop using a damaged or worn head. Follow the exact model's water symbols and cleaning instructions.
4. **Match the hair length to the manual.** For BRE708/BRE728, 3–4 mm is the supported range. If another manual gives no length, do not import Philips's number.
5. **Choose wet or dry correctly.** BRE708/BRE728 can be used on completely wet or completely dry skin. BRE227/00 is corded; do not use it in a bath, shower, or another wet-use setting unless its exact current instructions explicitly permit that use.
6. **Prepare only as the manual directs.** Do not add powder, numbing cream, antiseptic, acids, oil, or unverified deodorant rules.
7. **Raise the arm and stretch the skin.** The exact sources support stretching the underarm. Do not add a universal direction pattern beyond the exact model's instructions.
8. **Use the model's angle and cap.** BRE708/BRE728 use 75° or the ProGuide held flat. No exact BRE227 angle is approved from the captured sources. Do not publish one universal angle.
9. **Move slowly with light pressure.** Work against the visible direction of growth and adjust direction in the underarm where needed. Avoid repeatedly passing the same spot. Stop if the device pinches skin, the pain is abnormal, or the skin is injured.
10. **Clean the device exactly as directed.** Switch off or unplug first. Waterproof, washable-head, and non-washable rules are not interchangeable. Air-dry washable parts fully before reassembly where the manual requires it.
11. **Finish and assess the skin.** Finish according to the manual and assess the skin before applying any aftercare. Keep detailed device cleaning, manufacturer-qualified lotion/deodorant guidance, and wound care in the dedicated aftercare section.

### Required stop boxes

Place the stop rule twice: once before the procedure and again beside the movement/pressure steps.

> **Stop before you begin:** Do not proceed if the manual says not to use the device on the current skin condition or situation. Stop if epilation cuts the skin, causes bleeding, or produces unusual, severe, or worsening pain.

> **Pause before switching sides:** Do not continue automatically if the first underarm is bleeding, cut, becoming progressively more irritated, or causing unusual, severe, or worsening pain. Treat each side independently; a different reaction on one side does not establish a diagnosis.

## Reaction wording and stop signs

### Proposed wording for approval

- Epilation can be followed by temporary itchiness, redness, tightness, small bumps, or a burning sensation according to Philips support applying to BRE227/00 and related models.
- Do not promise a precise recovery time for everyone. Philips advises medical consultation if irritation lasts longer than three days, but the article should also tell readers to seek help sooner for severe, worsening, or infection-like symptoms.
- **Stop and provide basic first aid:** If the skin is cut or bleeding, stop epilating. Apply direct pressure with clean gauze or a clean cloth, clean a minor cut, and cover it as appropriate, following US AAD/MedlinePlus guidance.
- **Seek prompt medical advice:** Contact an appropriate clinician for worsening pain, increasing redness or swelling, warmth, pus-like drainage, fever, chills, or feeling unwell. MedlinePlus and AAD are the primary US authorities; NHS guidance is supplementary.
- **Seek urgent help:** Seek urgent medical help for severe bleeding, bleeding that does not stop with firm direct pressure, or a severe or rapidly worsening reaction.
- Ingrown hairs may occur after plucking-type hair removal. Do not scratch, pick, or squeeze them. Seek care if the area becomes very painful, hot, swollen, or is accompanied by fever or feeling unwell.
- The article may describe warning signs and appropriate next actions, but it must not identify the reader's condition from symptoms or photographs.

## Why hair can be visible again within days

The article should avoid inventing a single biological explanation. The evidence supports a decision tree:

- **Missed hair:** The head may not have caught every visible hair during the prior session.
- **Hair outside the usable length range:** Hair can be too short to catch or long enough to reduce efficient pickup; follow the exact manual.
- **Breakage or poor pickup:** A dirty/damaged head, excessive pressure, incorrect angle, fast movement, or poor skin tension can reduce performance according to Philips support. Do not state that every visible hair is broken.
- **Different visible hairs:** Hair that was below the skin or too short during the prior session may become visible later. Do not assign an exact growth-cycle schedule without stronger evidence.
- **Marketing ceiling:** `Up to four weeks` or similar language is a manufacturer maximum, not an individual guarantee.
