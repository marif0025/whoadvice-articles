## Reader decision

The reader needs to match a trimmer to hedge size, stem thickness, reach, available outlets, and any battery platform already owned. A satisfactory guide must distinguish blade length from cut capacity, explain the full weight and reach tradeoffs, and keep hedge trimmers separate from tools for thick branches.

## Search and competitor findings

Current results favor ranked buying guides with cordless, corded, and long-reach options. Gardeners' World, HGTV, and Consumer Reports are the strongest editorial competitors located. Their useful coverage includes power formats, blade length, handling, and cutting capacity. The main WhoAdvice opportunity is exact-model normalization with regional bundle notes, battery-platform costs, explicit recall exclusions, and community-led questions about cords, tall hedges, and whether voltage alone predicts performance.

## Exclusions

- RYOBI RY40620VNM, RY40602VNM, and RY40602BTLVNM: affected serial ranges were recalled by CPSC in June 2025.
- EGO HT2410 manufactured before March 30, 2019: subject to a 2023 CPSC recall; it is not the HT2601 selected here.
- DEWALT DCHT820B/DCHT820P1: manufacturer catalog marks the older generation discontinued. The selected DCHT821B is the current bare-tool package verified for this roundup.
- Gas models: retained in category education, but no current exact model offered a clearer home-garden use case than the selected electric tools within the evidence gathered.
