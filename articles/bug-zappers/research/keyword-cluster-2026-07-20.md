## Executive decision

WhoAdvice already has seven related production URLs. Treat this as a coordinated **existing-cluster refresh**, not a greenfield five-page launch. The supplied assessment identified the existing pages; a July 20 production check confirmed content for all seven. The pillar, indoor, solar, Flowtron, and mosquito-killer pages returned raw text, while the outdoor and racket pages were confirmed through current search-indexed production content after raw text opens returned errors.

Keep the broad **best bug zapper** page as the commercial pillar, but make its promise insect-specific. The CSV contains strong demand for mosquito wording, while current university-extension guidance says conventional UV-only zappers do not reduce mosquito biting rates. Keyword volume therefore cannot justify overlapping pages that imply reliable bite control.

The recommended commercial refresh order is:

1. **Do bug zappers work?** — create the evidence boundary before commercial rewrites.
2. **Best bug zapper** — refresh the broad pillar and format selector.
3. **Best indoor bug zapper** — indoor-only stationary devices and enclosed light traps.
4. **Best outdoor bug zapper** — weather exposure, outlet route, hanging location, cleaning, and non-mosquito use cases.
5. **Best bug zapper racket** — broaden secondary targeting to `best electric fly swatter` and keep handheld devices separate.
6. **Best solar bug zapper** — retain provisionally because the mapped solar family is substantial; fully reverify the shortlist.
7. **Best mosquito killer** — reposition as an alternative-based mosquito-control guide, not a zapper duplicate.
8. **Best Flowtron bug zapper** — keep only if current models and production performance justify a standalone brand page.

The cross-context assessment recommends retaining the existing solar page. That changes the earlier greenfield recommendation to consolidate solar into outdoor: keep the URL during the audit, then require exact-model, traffic, backlink, affiliate, and cannibalization evidence before the refreshed ranking is approved.

Do not create launch pages for brands, retailers, replacement bulbs, local purchasing terms, generic sprays, repellents, insect identification, DIY high-voltage builds, or every insect modifier.

### Competitor-overlap conclusion

The broad commercial SERP already absorbs many indoor/outdoor/solar/portable awards, but dedicated indoor and outdoor pages also rank. This supports a hub-and-branch structure with explicit shortlist boundaries:

- The pillar chooses among formats and may feature one representative use case per branch.
- Indoor owns enclosed rooms, stationary indoor devices, wall/outlet formats, noise, cleaning, and food-area limitations.
- Outdoor owns weather exposure, outlet/cord routing, hanging versus tabletop use, general flying-insect nuisance, and environmental tradeoffs.
- Electric fly swatters own active handheld contact control and safety features.
- The effectiveness guide owns which insects are attracted, mosquito-bite-control limits, non-target insect concerns, and expectation setting.

## Article-level evidence requirements

### Commercial pages

Run WhoAdvice Part 1 before any ranking or awards. Verify:

- exact model, variant, package, intended location, power source, and market;
- manufacturer page and manual;
- indoor/outdoor rating and weather limitations;
- bulb/LED serviceability, tray or grid cleaning, placement, and mounting instructions;
- grid guarding, switch/lock design, and relevant household warnings;
- current availability, retailer eligibility, recalls, and unresolved conflicts;
- the exact insect-attraction mechanism and whether mosquito language is only a brand claim.
- EPA establishment and device status without presenting it as EPA approval, safety certification, registration, or efficacy review; [EPA's consumer guide](https://www.epa.gov/pesticides/pesticide-devices-guide-consumers) says pesticidal devices generally do not undergo the pesticide premarket review and an establishment number does not indicate safety or efficacy approval.

Do not treat voltage, wattage, or advertised acreage as proven catch performance without comparable independent evidence.

### Effectiveness page

Use university extension, public-health, regulator, or peer-reviewed sources for insect behavior and mosquito-control claims. Separate:

- insects commonly attracted to light;
- direct-contact killing versus area-wide population or bite reduction;
- UV-only devices versus traps that add CO2, octenol, heat, fans, or sticky capture;
- indoor nuisance reduction versus outdoor mosquito control;
- target insects from non-target or beneficial insects.

## Internal-link architecture

- The pillar links to each commercial branch from the corresponding format decision.
- Each branch links back to the pillar for readers who chose the wrong format.
- Every commercial page links once to the effectiveness guide near its target-insect or expectation section.
- The effectiveness guide links to a commercial page only after explaining when the mechanism may fit.
- Indoor and outdoor pages may cross-link only for genuinely dual-rated models or an unresolved location decision.
- Outdoor links to the existing solar page only when the reader lacks reliable mains power or specifically wants solar charging; solar links back for readers whose location suits a plug-in model.
