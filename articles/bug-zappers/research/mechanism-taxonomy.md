# Bug-zapper mechanism taxonomy

**Status:** Locked for the topology audit
**Date:** July 20, 2026
**Applies to:** All bug-zapper cluster research, product records, briefs, drafts, and audits

## Editorial rule

Classify a product by how it attracts, captures, and kills an insect—not by the retailer category or a loose phrase such as “mosquito killer.” Use one primary mechanism and record secondary mechanisms separately. Do not use **bug zapper**, **mosquito killer**, **insect trap**, and **fly trap** as interchangeable technical labels.

| Controlled term | Operational definition | Included examples | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open electric grid zapper | Exposed electrified killing grid, normally paired with a light attractant | Traditional hanging grid unit | Do not imply the light attracts every insect or that kills equal population/bite reduction |
| Guarded electric grid zapper | Electrified grid behind an external finger/animal guard | Guarded lantern or stationary unit | A guard is a design feature, not proof of child, pet, fire, or shock safety |
| Enclosed indoor electric trap | Indoor electric-kill mechanism enclosed within a housing | Wall, countertop, or commercial-style enclosed unit | Separate from glue-board and fan capture; verify exact indoor and food-area instructions |
| UV glue-board trap | Light-attraction device that captures insects on an adhesive board | Indoor plug-in or wall-mounted glue-card trap | It does not “zap”; replacement-board compatibility belongs in the exact product record |
| Fan-assisted trap | Fan draws insects into a chamber, net, or retaining basket | Indoor countertop or outdoor fan trap | Do not treat advertised capture as area-wide control; record any light/heat/lure separately |
| Solar-powered grid zapper | Solar-charged device whose killing mechanism is an electric grid | Solar lantern/grid product | Solar describes the power source; verify charging, battery, weather, and runtime claims |
| Handheld electric racket | User actively contacts an insect with an electrified racket/grid | Rechargeable fly swatter or mosquito bat | Not an unattended attract-and-kill device unless a separately verified base/light mode exists |
| Lure-assisted mosquito trap | Trap using CO2, heat, odor, octenol, or another mosquito cue, possibly with a fan | CO2-, heat-, odor-, or octenol-assisted system | Adjacent mosquito-control mechanism, not a conventional UV-only zapper; evaluate the exact system |

## Required product fields

Every normalized exact-product record must state:

- brand, exact model, variant, and package;
- primary mechanism from the table above;
- secondary attraction/capture mechanisms;
- intended indoor/outdoor location and verified environmental rating;
- power source;
- target insects claimed by the manufacturer, clearly labeled as manufacturer claims;
- manual and manufacturer sources;
- cleaning/service method and consumables;
- guards, switches, locks, mounting, cord, and placement instructions;
- current availability and any source conflicts.

## Language controls

- “Kills insects” may describe direct contact/capture only when the mechanism or product evidence supports it.
- “Controls,” “protects,” “reduces,” and acreage/coverage language require evidence for the stated real-world outcome.
- Never infer mosquito-bite reduction from insect counts, grid voltage, wattage, UV wavelength, or advertised coverage.
- “Chemical-free,” “non-toxic,” “eco-friendly,” “safe,” “humane,” and “bug-free” are not default descriptors.
- An EPA establishment number is not EPA approval, registration, safety certification, or efficacy review.
- For “gnats,” identify the insect group and breeding source before discussing a fitting control mechanism.
