## Strategic decision

WhoAdvice already has seven related production pages under `/home-gadgets/`. Keep those canonical paths during the initial audit. The immediate job is to establish strict ownership, correct unsupported effectiveness language, normalize mechanisms, reverify exact products, and decide whether the Flowtron page still deserves an independent URL.

Use a new **do bug zappers work?** guide as the evidence foundation for the commercial refreshes. Conventional UV-only zappers may kill attracted insects, but authoritative extension guidance does not support them as a reliable way to reduce mosquito biting rates. The existing mosquito-killer page must therefore become an alternative-based mosquito-control guide, not another zapper roundup.

## Ownership boundaries

- **Pillar:** mixed-format selection, short representative shortlist, and branch routing.
- **Indoor:** enclosed-room use, stationary indoor formats, noise, guarding, cleaning, and indoor placement limits.
- **Outdoor:** plug-in grid zappers, weather exposure, hanging/tabletop use, outlet route, and environmental tradeoffs.
- **Solar:** solar-powered outdoor products only; no general outdoor ranking duplication.
- **Rackets:** handheld active-contact devices; target `best electric fly swatter` as the main secondary term.
- **Flowtron:** current Flowtron model-family comparison only; consolidate into outdoor if it lacks independent value.
- **Mosquito killer:** traps, larval control, repellents, and appropriate alternatives; do not present conventional UV zappers as reliable bite control.
- **Effectiveness:** attraction mechanism, insect-by-insect limits, mosquito expectations, non-target insects, and the difference between killing an insect and reducing bites or a population.
