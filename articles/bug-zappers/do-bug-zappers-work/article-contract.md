## Reader and opening contract

The reader sees insects being killed and wants to know whether that visible activity solves the actual problem. Answer immediately:

- Electric-grid zappers can kill insects that contact the grid.
- Conventional outdoor UV-only zappers have not been shown to reduce mosquito biting rates.
- Results for flies and small indoor insects depend on species, source, setting, placement, competing light, and capture mechanism.
- Outdoor zappers can kill non-target insects.

The opening must define “work” by outcome. It must not mention a product, retailer, award, affiliate link, study percentage, or national insect-mortality estimate.

## FAQ answer boundaries

- **Attract more mosquitoes:** “A conventional UV light may draw some nearby insects toward the device, and individual mosquitoes may approach it. The evidence does not establish that this increases mosquitoes across the yard. Conventional outdoor UV-only zappers also have not been shown to reduce biting rates.”
- **Mosquito-free patio:** No. Do not convert a catch or kill into a bite-free or mosquito-free claim.
- **Indoor flies:** Some indoor UV traps may capture some flies under suitable conditions; species, placement, competing light, sanitation, and capture design matter.
- **Fungus gnats:** A trap may catch adults, but moisture and potting-media source management remain necessary.
- **EPA establishment number:** It identifies the producing establishment; it is not EPA approval of safety or efficacy.

## Public wording exclusions

Use this reader-facing mosquito sentence:

> Conventional outdoor UV-only zappers have not been shown to reduce mosquito biting rates.

Do not publish internal status labels such as “Evidence supports no meaningful effect.” Do not say:

- bug zappers have no effect on mosquitoes;
- they never attract mosquitoes;
- they cannot kill mosquitoes;
- science proves they are completely useless;
- a conventional zapper increases the mosquito population across a yard;
- kills or catch counts establish fewer landings, bites, mosquitoes, or disease risk;
- lure-assisted mosquito traps all work or all fail;
- a device selectively avoids bees or is safe for pollinators, children, pets, food areas, or the environment by default.

Required food-area wording:

> Do not place an open-grid electrocuting trap near exposed food or food-preparation areas.

Required mechanism comparison:

> Glue-board traps and fan-only traps do not use the same electrocution mechanism. Their sanitation, placement, and cleaning requirements still depend on the exact design and manual.
