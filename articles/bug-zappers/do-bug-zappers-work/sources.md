# Do bug zappers work? — source register

**Research date:** July 20, 2026  
**Market:** United States  
**Evidence model:** Evidence-led informational explainer; no WhoAdvice hands-on testing, products, rankings, awards, retailer links, or affiliate links

These sources support the Part 3 draft. Reopen all sources used for public-health, mosquito, non-target, food-area, and regulatory claims before publication.

## Mosquito outcomes and prevention

- Colorado State University Extension, “West Nile Virus and Mosquito Management”: https://extension.colostate.edu/resource/west-nile-virus-and-mosquito-management/
  - Supports the conventional UV-only biting-rate boundary, non-target killing, mosquito host cues, and the distinction between UV-only and CO2/octenol-assisted traps.
  - Does not establish that every lure-assisted system succeeds or fails in every environment.
- University of Kentucky Entomology, “Mosquitoes: Practical Advice for Homeowners”: https://entomology.mgcafe.uky.edu/ef005
  - Supports the limits of outdoor UV-only zappers for mosquito populations and biting activity and the need to evaluate other trap systems cautiously.
  - General homeowner guidance, not an exact-product trial.
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, “Preventing Mosquito Bites”: https://www.cdc.gov/mosquitoes/prevention/index.html
  - Supports EPA-registered repellents used as directed, protective clothing, screens and air conditioning, and weekly management of water-holding containers.
  - Prevention guidance does not promise zero bites or zero disease risk.
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, “Integrated Mosquito Management”: https://www.cdc.gov/mosquitoes/php/toolkit/integrated-mosquito-management-1.html
  - Supports mosquito management as a layered program rather than a single-device outcome.

## Indoor flies and small flying insects

- Texas A&M AgriLife Extension, “Indoor Flies and Their Control”: https://agrilifeextension.tamu.edu/library/insects/indoor-flies-and-their-control/
  - Supports UV attraction to glue boards or low-voltage grids, stronger fit for larger flies such as house flies, and the warning that high-voltage zappers can scatter fragments indoors.
  - General indoor-fly guidance, not an exact-product trial.
- Oregon State Extension, “Integrated Pest Management of Nonbiting Flies in Schools”: https://extension.oregonstate.edu/catalog/pnw-770-integrated-pest-management-nonbiting-flies-schools
  - Supports indoor UV sticky-board traps, the importance of placement without competing light, and avoiding electrocution traps that can contaminate indoor food areas.
  - School IPM context; do not transfer its conclusions to every fly species or product.
- Urban and Broce, “Killing of Flies in Electrocuting Insect Traps Releases Bacteria and Viruses,” Current Microbiology 41(4), 2000: https://doi.org/10.1007/s002840010132
  - Reports that high-voltage grids disintegrated house flies and released particles and microorganisms during electrocution.
  - Controlled study; it does not establish identical exposure or risk for every device and environment.
- UC Integrated Pest Management, “Flies”: https://ipm.ucanr.edu/PMG/PESTNOTES/pn7457.html
  - Supports sanitation, exclusion, and breeding-site removal.
  - The current page does not support the prior claim that indoor light traps help particularly where daylight does not compete.
- University of Minnesota Extension, “Managing insects on indoor plants”: https://extension.umn.edu/yard-and-garden-insects/insects-indoor-plants
  - Supports fungus-gnat association with moist potting media, moisture management, and sticky-card capture of adults.
- UC Integrated Pest Management, “Fungus Gnats”: https://ipm.ucanr.edu/home-and-landscape/fungus-gnats/
  - Supports fungus-gnat biology and the limit of adult-only capture.
- Penn State Extension, “Moth Flies in the Home”: https://extension.psu.edu/moth-flies-in-the-home
  - Supports drain/moth-fly identity and moist organic breeding sources.
- UC Integrated Pest Management, “Fruit Flies”: https://ipm.ucanr.edu/home-and-landscape/fruit-flies/
  - Supports fruit-fly identification and management context.
- University of Minnesota Extension, “Fruit flies”: https://apps.extension.umn.edu/garden/diagnose/insect/indoor/flies/small/fruit-flies.html
  - Supports fruit-fly identification and association with fermenting food and liquid sources.
- University of Minnesota Extension, “Chironomid midges”: https://apps.extension.umn.edu/garden/diagnose/insect/general-curiosity-insects/flies/small/chironomidmidges.html
  - Supports the distinction between non-biting midges and mosquitoes and notes light attraction.

## Moths, bees, wasps, and non-target insects

- Penn State Extension, “Moths—The Forgotten Pollinators”: https://extension.psu.edu/moths-the-forgotten-pollinators
  - Supports moths as a distinct group, their pollinator role, and artificial-light interference.
  - Does not quantify zapper mortality or establish a moth-specific kill rate.
- University of Georgia Extension, “Insect and Arthropod Pests of Southeastern Neighborhoods”: https://fieldreport.caes.uga.edu/publications/B1412/insect-and-arthropod-pests-of-southeastern-neighborhoods/
  - Supports bee and wasp distinctions and beneficial roles.
  - Does not quantify bee entry into zappers or bee mortality.
- University of Georgia Extension, “Stinging and Biting Pests”: https://extension.uga.edu/publications/detail.cfm?number=C782
  - Supports species- and nest-based management of bees and wasps.
  - Does not establish UV attraction or zapper efficacy.

## Regulatory source

- US Environmental Protection Agency, “Pesticide Devices: A Guide for Consumers”: https://www.epa.gov/pesticides/pesticide-devices-guide-consumers
  - Supports the meaning of an EPA establishment number and the boundary that it is not product approval, registration, certification, endorsement, or EPA safety/efficacy review.

## Claim and wording register

| Claim area | Strongest source | Public wording limit |
|---|---|---|
| Conventional outdoor UV-only zappers and mosquito bites | Colorado State + Kentucky Extension | Have not been shown to reduce mosquito biting rates; individual kills do not establish later outcomes |
| Lure-assisted mosquito traps | Colorado State Extension | Evaluate the exact system; a larger catch does not establish fewer landings, bites, or a smaller local population |
| Indoor fly capture | Texas A&M + Oregon State Extension | Some traps may capture larger flies such as house flies; species, setting, competing light, sanitation, and mechanism matter |
| Open grids near food | Texas A&M + Oregon State + Urban and Broce | Do not place an open-grid electrocuting trap near exposed food or food-preparation areas; do not generalize exposure or risk across every device |
| Fungus gnats | Minnesota Extension + UC IPM | Adult capture does not resolve moist potting-media breeding sources |
| Drain and fruit flies | Penn State + UC IPM | Start with source identification and removal; no generic “gnat zapper” conclusion |
| Moths and non-target insects | Penn State + Colorado State | Moths are distinct and may be pollinators; do not quantify zapper mortality from this packet |
| Bees and wasps | UGA Extension | Do not infer selective avoidance, safety, attraction, entry, or mortality |
| EPA establishment number | US EPA | Production-establishment identifier, not product approval or safety/efficacy review |

## Excluded claims and sources

- No historical catch percentage or national beneficial-insect mortality estimate is used in the draft.
- The University of Minnesota black-cutworm source is not used for general moth claims.
- No product page, manufacturer target list, advertised coverage, voltage, wattage, UV wavelength, or catch count is used to establish control.
- No commercial competitor, retailer, marketplace, or community source is used as public proof.
